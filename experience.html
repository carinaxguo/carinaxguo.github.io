Experience

Research

Student Researcher under Dr. Jonathan Gruber and Dr. Rena Conti (June 2021 - Present)

I'm currently working with Dr. Jonathan Gruber of MIT to update the 7th Edition of his “Public Finance and Public Policy” textbook, the most-used public finance textbook in America. I'm also conducting analysis and compiling health insurance and retirement savings statistics of American workers.

I'm also working with Dr. Rena Conti of Boston University, extracting and analyzing 500+ COVID vaccine clinical trial statistics on location, vaccine platforms, agreements, etc. I also researched Securities and Exchange Commission company filings to investigate the impacts of compulsory licensing, manufacturing contracts, and IP agreements on COVID vaccine development.

Harvard International Economics Essay Contest (August 2020 - December 2020)

Throughout the latter half of 2020, I conducted a meta-analysis and survey of existing research literature on the groupthink phenomenon, a subject within behavioral economics that occurs when the desire of harmony within a group overpowers standard reason and rational decision making. I summarized my findings in an essay: "The Big Brother Watching Us Today: Groupthink”.

Wharton Global High School Investment Competition (August 2020 - December 2020)

Alongside my teammates over the course of ten weeks, I developed my own investment portfolio strategy (nicknamed COMPASS) after reaching out to real-life trading experts and competing with a stock market simulator. In this analysis, I go over the strategy's stock-selecting methodology, as well as how it balances four factors (News, Earnings, World, and Stability).

Academic Programs

University of Pennsylvania’s Management & Technology Summer Institute (July 2021)

At M&TSI, I was taught by Penn’s Wharton and SEAS professors alongside 90 students about various topics in the intersection between business, technology, and entrepreneurship. I studied Harvard Business School company case studies (LinkedIn, Dropbox, IDEO, etc.), attended seminars with entrepreneurs (Strella Biotech, Niantic, Lia, etc.), and developed my own product prototypes with Arduino circuits. After self-teaching basic C++, I created the circuit prototype, developed the website, and wrote the executive summary for our product: FulPillment, a synced app and pillbox duo that automatically keeps track of pill-taking and reminds the user to take/reorder them.
